About Us

https://rewritingthecode.org/

Rewriting the Code is a global community that strives to empower women in tech. As the largest peer-to-peer network for undergraduate, graduate, and early career women in tech, Rewriting the Code offers continuous engagement with tech companies, personal and professional skill development, and mentorship. RTC paves the way for sustainable careers, professional advancement, and equal opportunities for the next generation of tech leaders. We connect women with engineers and resources to sustain a career in tech. We foster relationships with industry experts and illuminate opportunities that stipulate a universal support network and open doors for career advancement. We facilitate year-round access and needed support with technical, professional, and personal development, as well as broad industry and career path exploration, recruiting, and mentorship. Culture, Misson, and Core Values

OUR MISSION

Rewriting the Code supports and empowers college, graduate and early career women in tech, through intersectional communities, mentorship, industry experience and educational resources, to become the next generation of engineers and tech leaders.

OUR CORE VALUES

Belonging and Community : Belonging is our bedrock. Community is our charge.

Empowerment : We lift, lean on, and leverage each other's strengths.Access should have no admission fee.

Impact : We bravely disrupt inequity to boldly impact the future.

Representation: We create mirrors and shatter glass ceilings.

What Do We Do?

We create programs that college women in tech say they need, such as:

Community: Impactful community engagement with peers, mentors, companies and more

Career: Understanding of possible career paths and opportunities for students pursuing computer science and engineering degrees.

Broader Exposure: Exposure to technology disciplines such as artificial intelligence, natural language processing, machine learning, blockchain, data science & cybersecurity, and applications across industries.

Mentorship: Advice from a broad network of company tech leaders, near peers, mentors, and women role models.

Opportunity: Opportunities to apply technical skills in a work environment through internships.

Skill Development: Personal skill development including networking, confidence, communication, leadership, and navigating challenging situations.
